About me

Name: Chris Brislin
Age: 29
Location: Sydney, Australia
Cats: 2

"I like things that fly"

Like a lot of commercial pilots, my passion for flight began young, at age 10 with model aircraft. I was fortunate enough to represent Australia on the world stage of model aerobatics several times, ultimately placing 2nd at the world championships in 2009.

Fast forward to today, I am a commercial pilot flying Boeing 737's around Australia with several thousand hours of aeronautical experience. I am passionate about the development of unmanned aircraft and believe they will play a huge role in the future of many industries.

This passion for unmanned aircraft and their development lead me to develop an interest in programming, and 2020 afforded me the time to persue some formalised skills in the form of a Diploma of Software Development. The Diploma focussed on mobile app development and Java, but has left me with some core programming skills applicable to many languages.

After my start in aviation with model aircraft, and a career in commercial flying, it was only natural that I began to gravitate towards an amalgamation of those two worlds: unmanned aircraft. Projects

Swarm Controller

This is the first significant software project I have tackled in Java. It is designed to work with large swarms of small drones used for light shows. This software is intended to function as a very high-level backup control interface for each drone. It utilises the Mavlink communication protocol to communicate via a serial link to each individual drone, or allow and operator to control multiple drones at once.



Android Colour Picker

My first completed Android application developed for my Diploma of Software development. It is a basic colour picker that generates HTML colour codes. Keep and eye out for my future Android projects!
